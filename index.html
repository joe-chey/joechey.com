Kinetic Sound, link
rebranding classical music as a genre

Committed to creating polished instrumental music productions for the millennials, Kinetic Sound attracted top student talents in music, design, marketing, strategy, and finance, and organized some of the most successful student-led instrumental music concerts at the University of Virginia. After gathering more than 140 students at its first concert in 2015 - just 3 months after the organization was founded with 3 members - Kinetic Sound continuously refined its priorities and strategies based on user interviews, presenting an outdoor instrumental music concert attended by over 230 student audience members in 2017.

Re:Construct, link
communicating the abstract

With over 2,000 views on social media, this multidisciplinary performing arts project is a modern reinterpretation of the tradition and culture representing the University of Virginia. In addition to creating and managing the project roadmap from beginning to completion, I pitched the project before the UVA Bicentennial Committee, as well as at a selective crowdfunding event to secure and manage $4,000 in various institutional sponsorships.

花は咲く
tapping different possibilities for value

In 2015, I initiated contact with a visiting calligraphy master from Japan with an inquiry regarding a public demonstration of her crafts for a Japanese culture event I was organizing. After weeks of written and in-person negotiation with the calligrapher, community members, and the event staff, I successfully curated a unique professional calligraphy performance open to public at no charge, where the calligrapher dynamically produced calligraphy pieces with the lyrics of a live song that inspired her real-time.
