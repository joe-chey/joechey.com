communicating the abstract

A vague vision of a design concept, with the University's 200-year-old school song rebranded in cutting-edge modernity, performed by a group of students dressed in avant-garde fashion, and played on piano, electric bass, and 4 trash cans. In presenting this concept to prospective members, partners, and sponsors, I was able to gain a deep experience in communicating a truly abstract idea to stakeholders for mutual understanding.
I worked tirelessly to convey this concept to a number of audiences in writing, phone, and presentations in front of crowds as large as 150 people at a selective crowdfunding event, and successfully secured $4,000 in institutional sponsorships. With the funding and close collaboration with a designer, I was able to spearhead the creation of a multidisciplinary film product that captured the complex layers of developments experienced by University students and community over time.
